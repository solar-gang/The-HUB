Approach Training (Non-Solar Homes)

Simple flow • Audio-first • Tap to reveal scripts
Part 1

Intro

Goal: credibility + permission
Key line “Has Edison come by, or has anyone talked to you about the changes they made?”
Show Script

“Hey, how’s it going? Is this the Johnson residence?

Perfect — you must be Steve. Awesome. I’m Carlos.

I’m actually working on a lot of the power projects here in the area. You’ve probably noticed those small white boxes popping up on the side of some homes.

The reason they’re doing that — and the reason we’re out here — is because of the Time-of-Use changes. Has Edison come by, or has anyone talked to you about the changes they made?”

Why this works Low pressure. You’re not pitching—just checking what they’ve been told. That keeps the door open.
Part 2

Problem

Default: Edison grid upgrades → costs passed to homeowners
Key line “Even if your habits don’t change, the bill can still climb because the pricing keeps moving.”
Show Script

“Edison’s been doing major grid upgrades and infrastructure work, and the cost gets passed back to homeowners through rate increases.

So even if your habits don’t change, the bill can still climb because the pricing keeps moving.

Have you noticed that at all?”

Why this works It removes blame from the homeowner. Most people will agree quickly and keep listening.

Part 3

Solution

Default: VPP program
Key line “Store power and use it when the grid is stressed.”
Show Script

“A big part of the solution is storage, and that’s where the VPP program comes in.

It’s designed to help homeowners store power and use it when the grid is stressed—so you’re not stuck paying the worst pricing when everyone needs electricity.

It also gives you outage protection when the grid goes down.”

Why this works It sounds logical (not salesy): you match the exact problem they just agreed with.
Part 4

Qualification + Savings

Goal: set rules + build trust
Key line “We must be able to provide you savings on day one — otherwise, we can’t help you.”
Show Script

“My job is really simple — it’s to find out which homes might qualify for the program.

We make sure you have a good roof, that you get enough sunlight, and that you can produce the power.

We also make sure we can store that power for you.

If everything checks out and looks good, we’re able to provide a savings report. That report shows you what kind of savings you would have starting day one.

There’s nothing you have to pay out of pocket and nothing due upfront.

You must be able to qualify for the program, and we must be able to provide you savings on day one — otherwise, we can’t help you.

Here’s a sample bill from one of our customers. This is their July payment. As you can see, it’s only about twenty dollars.

That amount is just the base fee for staying connected to the grid. They’re not paying for power production or generation because they’re able to produce it and store it on-site.

Typically, how do you pay your power bill — online or by paper?”

Why this works This prevents wasted time and increases trust: “If we can’t show savings, we stop.”
Part 5

Close (Bill Snapshot)

Goal: micro-commitment
Key line “Perfect. Can you grab that real quick?”
Show Script

“I just need to grab a quick snapshot of that bar graph, like the one I showed you on my bill. It shows us how much power you use throughout the year.

From there, our engineers can take a look at it and see exactly whether or not we can help you.

Again, do you pay your bill online or by paper?

Perfect. Can you grab that real quick?”

Why this works You’re asking for a small action, not a decision. That’s an easy yes.
Part 6

Wrap-Up + Schedule

Goal: two options + trust exit
Key line “Which one works better for you?”
Show Script

“We’re in the area helping people today. We can come back in about 45 minutes to an hour if that works for you, or we can come back tomorrow around this same time.

Which one works better for you?

Perfect. I’ll send you a quick text right now.

If for some reason we’re not able to provide you any savings upfront, you’ll receive a text letting you know that we’ll cancel the appointment and disqualify the home.

And if anything changes on your end, please just shoot us a text and let us know ahead of time.

Other than that, we’ll see you tomorrow.”

Why this works Two options keep it moving without pressure. The cancel rule builds trust.
